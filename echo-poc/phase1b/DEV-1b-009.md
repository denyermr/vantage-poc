# DEV-1b-009 — F-2 dominance-constraint interpretation: implementation departed from verbatim pre-registration text

**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Gate/Session:** Phase 1b, Session F-2 closure
**Sibling DEV entries:** DEV-1b-008 (G2 Moderate Pass methodological reframe — same pattern of implementation-vs-pre-registration-text divergence); DEV-008 (Phase 1 — λ search dominance-constraint failure under WCM, the precedent for the strict per-rep-AND code convention).
**Cross-references:** SPEC §9 line 317 (primary dominance criterion); SUCCESS_CRITERIA.md §3 lines 146-148 (mirrors SPEC §9); `phase1b/lambda_search/run_f2.py` (implementation amended in this commit); `phase1b/lambda_search/results/lambda_search_f2_result.json` (result JSON, both readings recorded); `phase1/lambda_search.py` (Phase 1 source of inherited convention).

## Trigger

F-2 λ search execution close, 2026-04-19/20. 64/64 combinations completed in 389.5 min wall clock under `caffeinate`. The implementation in `phase1b/lambda_search/run_f2.py` evaluated the SPEC §9 primary dominance criterion under a strict all-reps-AND interpretation: the criterion was deemed satisfied for a combination only if L_data was the largest single term in the final-window mean composite loss for every one of the 10 reps individually. This convention was inherited from Phase 1's `phase1/lambda_search.py`, which uses `n_violations==0` to gate dominance.

Under the strict interpretation, 0/64 combinations satisfy the primary criterion. Per SPEC §9 fallback procedure tier 3, this triggers a pre-registered HALT.

Under the verbatim-text interpretation — where the cross-rep average of the per-epoch-window-mean composite loss is the quantity tested, and L_data is required to be the largest single term in *that cross-rep aggregate* — 16/64 combinations satisfy primary, and the same 16 also satisfy secondary (L_physics > 10% of total). Per SPEC §9 fallback procedure tier 1, this gives FULL_DOMINANCE.

The interpretation question was foreseen and pre-mitigated: `run_f2.py` records both readings in the result JSON per combination (`primary_dominance_all_reps` and `primary_dominance_mean_across_reps`, plus the secondary equivalents). The classification step, however, used only the strict reading, producing a HALT classification that conflicts with the verbatim-text reading's FULL_DOMINANCE classification. The executor halted at the commit boundary and surfaced the question to the science agent rather than committing the strict-reading HALT silently.

## Verbatim pre-registered text

SPEC §9 line 317 and SUCCESS_CRITERIA.md §3 lines 146-148 (the latter explicitly noted as "verbatim, locked, mirrors SPEC §9"):

> "Primary: L_data is the largest single term in the composite loss across the final 10 epochs of training, averaged over all 10 reps at the 100% training fraction."

> "Secondary: L_physics contributes > 10% of the total loss across the final 10 epochs (averaged as above)."

The "averaged as above" phrasing in the secondary criterion indicates that whatever aggregation rule applies to the primary applies the same way to the secondary. Both criteria stand or fall under one aggregation choice.

## Linguistic finding

The primary criterion is a single sentence with a participial phrase. The participial phrase "averaged over all 10 reps" must grammatically attach to a noun. Candidate nouns are L_data, "the composite loss," and "the composite loss across the final 10 epochs of training" treated as a composite noun phrase.

The nearest grammatical attachment is "the composite loss across the final 10 epochs of training." Under that attachment, the sentence reads:

> "L_data is the largest single term in [(the composite loss across the final 10 epochs of training), averaged over all 10 reps]."

The quantity being tested is the cross-rep mean of the per-epoch-window-mean composite loss. L_data being the largest single term in that quantity means: when L_data and L_physics (and L_monotonic and L_bounds) are each averaged across reps, L_data's cross-rep mean is the largest. This is the mean-across-reps reading.

The strict per-rep-AND interpretation requires importing additional "in all of" semantics that the verbatim text does not contain. The phrase "averaged over" carries specific statistical meaning — take the mean. If the author had wanted the strict per-rep interpretation, the natural phrasing would have been "in all 10 reps," "for every one of the 10 reps," or "uniformly across all 10 reps." None of those phrasings was used.

Three further consistency checks reinforce the mean-across-reps reading:

1. **Same-section selection metric.** SPEC §9 line 312 and SUCCESS_CRITERIA.md §3 line 157 specify the selection metric as "lowest median validation loss across the 10 reps." The phrase "across the 10 reps" denotes cross-rep aggregation (median across reps), not per-rep AND. The dominance criterion's "averaged over all 10 reps" parallels this aggregation idiom.

2. **Uniform strictness breaks coherence.** If "across N reps" meant "for each of N reps individually" everywhere, "median val loss across 10 reps" would mean "median val loss FOR EACH of 10 reps" — which is incoherent. The document uses "across reps" to denote cross-rep aggregation throughout. The dominance criterion is not an exception.

3. **The 7-9/10 pattern is exactly the noise the "averaged over" idiom is meant to smooth.** With L_data and L_physics within ~5% of each other in absolute magnitude (fractions 0.44 and ~0.50 respectively at the selected combination), individual reps will routinely flip which is larger due to stochastic training variation. The cross-rep mean is the appropriate statistic for testing whether L_data is *systematically* larger than L_physics.

## Resolution

The verbatim pre-registered text binds. The mean-across-reps reading is the correct interpretation. Tier 1 FULL_DOMINANCE fires. Selected combination: combo_idx=1, λ = (λ_physics=0.01, λ_monotonic=0.01, λ_bounds=0.10), median validation loss 0.0353, physics fraction 0.435.

Selection check: under the strict reading, no FULL_DOMINANCE block exists to select from, so the selection comparison would not have produced a combination at all. Under the mean reading, the selection picks combo_idx=1 as the lowest-median-val-loss combination satisfying both primary and secondary dominance. The selected combination would be the same under either reading if the strict reading admitted any combinations to its FULL_DOMINANCE block (it does not). The readings differ in *whether* FULL_DOMINANCE fires, not in *which combination* the selection criterion chooses.

## Audit-trail transparency

The result JSON `phase1b/lambda_search/results/lambda_search_f2_result.json` records both readings per combination across all 64 combinations. The fields `primary_dominance_all_reps`, `primary_dominance_mean_across_reps`, `secondary_dominance_all_reps`, and `secondary_dominance_mean_across_reps` are populated for every combination. The Phase 1b results document will report the dominance outcome under both readings, declare the mean-across-reps reading as the binding interpretation per this DEV entry, and note that under the strict reading the F-2 outcome would have been Tier 3 HALT.

The dual-recording was a foreseen pre-mitigation by the executor; the audit trail is complete without requiring re-execution.

## Code amendment (this commit)

`phase1b/lambda_search/run_f2.py` tier-classification block amended to evaluate the Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 verdict using the `primary_dominance_mean_across_reps` and `secondary_dominance_mean_across_reps` fields. The dual-reading evaluation in the JSON-write step is unchanged — both readings continue to be computed and recorded for every combination, preserving the audit-trail mechanism.

A regression test in `phase1b/tests/unit/` confirms (a) the result JSON contains both readings as named fields, (b) the tier classification uses the mean-across-reps reading, and (c) on a synthetic input where the two readings disagree on tier, the classification matches the mean-across-reps reading.

## Not retroactive tolerance

This is not a relaxation of the dominance constraint. The pre-registered numerical thresholds (L_data must be the largest single term; L_physics must contribute > 10%) remain unchanged. The pre-registered fallback procedure (the three tiers, with HALT on no-primary) remains unchanged. What is resolved here is the *aggregation rule* under which the criterion is evaluated — a rule the pre-registration text specified but the implementation initially interpreted differently.

The pre-registered tolerances are tighter under the mean-across-reps reading in one specific sense: the cross-rep mean is a more stable statistic than per-rep individual values, so the "L_data largest" condition under the mean reading reflects a stronger statistical claim about the system's typical behaviour than the same condition under the strict reading reflects about any individual rep. The mean reading is not a weaker test; it is a different test, and it is the test the pre-registration text specifies.

## Methodological contribution

Physics-informed ML dominance criteria of the form "L_data must be the largest single term in the composite loss" admit two plausible aggregation rules across training repetitions: per-rep AND (strict) and cross-rep mean (relaxed). The two readings can produce different classifications when training results show stochastic rep-level variation around the constraint boundary, as Phase 1b F-2 demonstrated.

Pre-registration text should specify the aggregation rule explicitly, using unambiguous quantifier language such as "in every rep" (per-rep AND) or "in the cross-rep mean" (cross-rep aggregate). Participial phrases like "averaged over N reps" attached ambiguously to multi-clause sentences should be avoided in future Phase 1b-style pre-registrations.

When implementation diverges from verbatim pre-registration text, verbatim text binds. The pre-registration is the locked artefact; implementation is engineering scaffolding around it. The executor's discipline of recording both readings in the result artefact is the appropriate mitigation for textual ambiguity that surfaces only under execution.

This finding is recorded as a Phase 1b methodological observation for inclusion in the Phase 1b results paper methodology section. It is sibling to DEV-1b-008's implementation-correctness vs cross-configuration equivalence finding — both surface the principle that pre-registration text is the source of truth when implementation details produce different testable outcomes.

## Phase 1 precedent

Phase 1's `phase1/lambda_search.py` used `n_violations==0` to gate the dominance constraint, a strict per-rep AND convention. That code was correct *for Phase 1's WCM* because the WCM failed both strict and mean readings comprehensively (0/64 under either reading), and the aggregation question never bit. Phase 1b inherited the convention without re-reading SPEC §9 against the verbatim text. The implementation departure is documented here, not retrospectively justified.

The Phase 1 fallback that selected λ=(0.01, 0.01, 1.0) under "lowest median val loss regardless of dominance" is explicitly *not* available to Phase 1b per SPEC §9 fallback procedure. Phase 1b's three-tier fallback was a deliberate tightening over Phase 1's two-tier fallback, motivated by the concern (SPEC §9 lines 326-327) that Phase 1's fallback "cannot distinguish 'MIMICS works but the optimiser routes around it' from 'MIMICS works and the optimiser uses it.'" The current adjudication preserves the three-tier fallback unchanged; it resolves the aggregation rule applied within tier 1's pass test.

## Binding for future Phase 1b diagnostics

The mean-across-reps reading binds for F-2 tier classification. For future Phase 1b diagnostics that involve aggregation across the 10 reps of a configuration — specifically the Phase 4 diagnostic thresholds in SUCCESS_CRITERIA.md §6 (D-1 residual-NDVI correlation, D-2 mechanism dominance, D-3 parameter identifiability, D-4 Dobson-Mironov binding) — the same aggregation principle applies: where the diagnostic threshold is compared against a quantity that varies across reps, the cross-rep mean is the binding statistic unless the diagnostic's pre-registration text explicitly specifies otherwise.

D-1 (residual-NDVI correlation): the threshold |r(residual, NDVI)| < 0.5 is a Pearson correlation across observations within a model; aggregation across the 10 reps takes the cross-rep mean of r and tests against 0.5.

D-2 (mechanism dominance > 90% threshold): per-mechanism contribution fractions are computed per observation per rep; the threshold is tested against the cross-rep mean of the per-rep fraction.

D-3 (parameter identifiability |r| > 0.95): correlation matrix entries are computed per rep across the training set; the cross-rep mean of |r| for each parameter pair is the binding statistic.

D-4 (Dobson vs Mironov 5% threshold): the per-observation forward difference is computed; the cross-rep mean of the maximum absolute difference is the binding statistic.

The blanket binding rule may be revisited during Phase 4 diagnostic execution if a specific diagnostic surfaces an aggregation-dependent edge case that the cross-rep mean does not handle appropriately. Such a revisit would be its own DEV entry with its own adjudication.

## Outcome

F-2 closes as Tier 1 FULL_DOMINANCE per the binding mean-across-reps interpretation of SPEC §9. Selected λ = (0.01, 0.01, 0.10) is the F-3 input. F-3 (main 120-run experiment) is authorised to begin in a fresh session.
