# DEV-1b-002 — Withdrawal of NDWI → m_g prior

**Date:** April 2026
**Status:** Resolved pre-sign-off (G2)
**Specification version affected:** Phase 1b SPEC.md v0.1-draft → v0.1
**Companion documents updated in parallel:** `vantage-mimics-litreview-v1_0_2.html` Decision 3 (promoted to v0.2 fallback) and Decision 5 (NDWI re-framed as diagnostic-only)
**Related deviation:** DEV-1b-001 (withdrew NDVI → LAI → N_l prior; same pre-sign-off cycle)

---

## Trigger

Pre-sign-off audit of the NDWI → m_g prior following DEV-1b-001's resolution, prompted by Claude Code's U-4 decision document. DEV-1b-001 had retained the NDWI prior on the grounds that its input-side physics (SWIR absorption by foliar water) was well-established even though the NDVI → LAI chain was not. U-4 asked whether that asymmetry survived scrutiny of the *output side* of the NDWI mapping.

## Finding

Two independent problems with the v0.1-draft NDWI → m_g prior, in descending order of force:

### Primary: The scalar-over-single-site structural issue

SPEC v0.1-draft §5 specified:

> "Per-observation NDWI mapped to a gravimetric moisture prior, with the same Gaussian envelope. Because m_g is treated as time-stable (single learned value across all observations), the prior is averaged across the training set."

The per-observation NDWI variation is averaged out *before* the prior is formed. The object actually seen by L_prior is a single scalar — the training-set-mean NDWI — used to inform a single scalar m_g_prior. At a single site (Moor House, N=83 training observations), the resulting training-set-mean NDWI is a single number that does not discriminate against any comparator site, any other time period, or any other vegetation state.

The "prior" is therefore not using NDWI as a time-varying signal; it is using NDWI to select a scalar. In information-theoretic terms, a single scalar drawn from training-set statistics at a single site carries no information beyond what a literature-chosen scalar would carry. NDWI's presence in the mapping gives the appearance of empirical grounding without providing any.

This is a structural defect in the v0.1-draft specification. It would remain a defect even if the output-side calibration problem below were solved.

### Secondary: No heather-specific NDWI → m_g calibration exists

No peer-reviewed empirical calibration mapping Gao-NDWI values to Calluna gravimetric moisture (g/g) is available. The literature offers:

- Gao (1996) defining NDWI and giving generic green-vegetation ranges (approximately −0.1 to 0.4).
- Crop-specific (wheat, soybean) and some site-specific (boreal spruce, evergreen oak) calibrations with destructive field sampling.
- Calluna xylem relative water content studies showing seasonal stability across British sites, but not published as a table of leaf+branch gravimetric values.
- No widely-cited table of "Calluna leaf+branch m_g = X ± Y g/g" tied to a remote-sensing index.

The SPEC v0.1-draft range [0.3, 0.6] g/g appears to be a literature-based envelope consistent with the branch gravimetric moisture range in the lit review §3 parameter table, but not calibrated against NDWI for this canopy.

NDWI's *input-side* physics (SWIR absorption by foliar water at B11 vs B08) is real and well-established. What is missing is the *output-side* calibration — the numerical mapping from Gao value to g/g for Calluna. A mapping invented for Phase 1b (whether linear, exponential, or piecewise) would sit in the same category as the generic-shrubland exponential NDVI → LAI substitute rejected in DEV-1b-001 Claude Code analysis.

### Tertiary: Methodological consistency with DEV-1b-001

DEV-1b-001 established the rule that the pre-registration does not invent transfer functions where no peer-reviewed calibration exists. Applying that rule symmetrically to the m_g case preserves methodological consistency; the pre-registration is stronger when its rules are applied uniformly. This is a supporting reason rather than the primary one, but it matters for the credibility of the Phase 1b pre-registration as a methodological artefact.

## Resolution

The NDWI → m_g prior is withdrawn. The L_prior term is removed entirely from the composite loss (§8), and λ_prior is no longer a model hyperparameter (§9). NDWI is retained as an *extracted* Sentinel-2 product (Gao 1996, using B08 and B11 bands, per U-3 work) but re-purposed as a **diagnostic-only covariate**. The §11 diagnostic plan is augmented with a post-experiment correlation check between training-set-mean NDWI and learned m_g (new Diagnostic D), which is the honest scientific use of the NDWI signal given no calibration exists: ask the post-hoc question "did the learned m_g correlate with an interpretable canopy-water signal?" rather than imposing a mapping in advance.

### Bound justification (pre-existing inconsistency resolved)

The lit review §3 parameter table gives separate ranges for leaf m_g (0.5–0.8 g/g) and branch m_g (0.3–0.6 g/g) with leaf and branch treated as distinct scatterers. SPEC v0.1-draft §5 adopted a single tied crown m_g using only the branch range (0.3–0.6), without explicit justification. This was a pre-existing inconsistency that became load-bearing once the NDWI prior was withdrawn — the bound choice now carries more regularisation weight.

**Resolution:** The branch range [0.3, 0.6] g/g is retained with explicit **branch-dominant-scatterer** justification. At C-band (λ ≈ 5.55 cm), Calluna branch radii (1–4 mm) are comparable to the wavelength and branches are the dominant volume scatterers; scale-like heather leaves (~0.2–0.3 cm) sit near the Rayleigh regime and contribute far less to the polarimetric signal. Tying m_g to the branch range is therefore physically defensible for a C-band retrieval. The leaf-range fraction of the moisture contribution is absorbed into the modelling bias budget rather than represented explicitly.

## Pre-registered v0.2 fallback

If post-experiment Diagnostic B (parameter correlation matrix) shows m_g is non-identifiable, or the m_g posterior pins to a bound, the documented v0.2 substitution is:

- **A season-stratified literature scalar prior** (summer m_g vs winter m_g), sourced from published Calluna water-relations field studies, with the prior operating as two scalars selected by a growing/dormant phenological categorical at each observation.
- **Wider Gaussian envelope** (σ ≥ 0.5 × m_g_prior), explicitly framed as sanity-bound regularisation rather than a retrieval target.
- **Not** a revival of any NDWI-derived mapping (linear, per-observation, or training-set-averaged). Not a retrieval of the original v0.1-draft formulation.

This fallback is adapted directly from lit review §8 Decision 3's recommendation — which was not adopted in SPEC v0.1 (simpler time-stable scalar chosen instead) but now has a defined path into the experiment if the simpler choice fails.

## R3 regularisation budget consequence (state plainly)

SPEC §9's tightened dominance constraint (L_physics > 10% of total loss, new for Phase 1b) was originally calibrated for a world with two Sentinel-2 priors carrying some of the structural-regularisation load. DEV-1b-001 removed one prior; DEV-1b-002 now removes the other. The §9 dominance constraint is, as of this amendment, the **sole structural regulariser** for the MIMICS parameter space (five learnable structural parameters plus per-observation m_v, against N=83 training observations).

This is not a fatal change. The §9 tightening was specifically introduced to absorb this kind of pressure. But the Phase 1b regularisation budget is materially tighter than v0.1-draft anticipated, and the §9 fallback procedure (primary-only if the secondary criterion fails) is a more loaded move than v0.1-draft described — because the secondary criterion was meant to be the backstop against the under-regularisation scenario that two withdrawn priors creates. If the secondary criterion fails during the λ search, the resulting configuration should be flagged as a Phase 1b finding (not a routine fallback), per the §9 logic as revised here.

This deviation and its sibling DEV-1b-001 are reported in the Phase 1b results document alongside the final outcome regardless of outcome direction, per §13 of the SPEC. Two withdrawals during a single pre-sign-off review is a meaningful signal about the honest-gates methodology in operation and should be described as such — not as a negative, but as the pre-registration doing its job before any training runs.
