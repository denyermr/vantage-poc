## Finding

**Every significant residual in the post-Phase-E-1b G2 result traces to a deliberate v0.1 simplification versus T94's implicit sub-module choice.** There are five distinct inherited-approximation mismatches between our v0.1 physics stack and T94's implicit reference configuration:

| # | v0.1 choice (our MIMICS) | T94's choice | Where it shows up in G2 residuals |
|---|---|---|---|
| (a) | Simplified power-law Dobson: ε = ε_dry + (ε_water − 1) · m_v^α with α = 0.65 | Full Dobson 1985 four-component mixing model (sand/silt/clay/bulk-density) | Gradient arm E.1 / E.2 ~7× shortfall |
| (b) | Oh 1992 empirical surface scattering (SPEC §7 primary for Moor House) | Physical-optics model (T94 §II.A, s = 0.55 cm, l_s = 4.9 cm, ks ≈ 0.65 within PO envelope) | Sets A.1 (2.55 dB), A.2 (1.96 dB), B.2 (1.91 dB); some contribution to C.1 (6.92 dB) |
| (c) | Rayleigh + single sinc² form factor for branch / leaf scattering amplitudes | Ulaby-Moore-Fung finite-cylinder form factors (Bessel radial, sinc axial, angle-averaged for extinction) | Set C.2 (11.16 dB); E.5 (7× off T94 with autograd = FD); contributes to A.4 / B.4 |
| (d) | Real-part-only Ulaby & El-Rayes (UEL) vegetation dielectric: ε_v = 1.7 + 18·m_g | Dual-dispersion UEL with Im(ε) | Secondary contributor to canopy-mechanism residuals; not individually dominant at Phase E-1b |
| (e) | √(σ°_oh) as a roughness-aware ground-reflectance proxy for crown-ground coupling | Literal Fresnel Γ with polarisation-specific reflectance | Brewster-region rows A.4 (6.54 dB), B.4 (7.97 dB) — VV-specific at θ = 50° |

These are **not implementation errors.** They are precisely the pre-registered v0.1→v0.2 promotion queue documented in the "Known limitations (v0.1)" block of `phase1b/physics/reference_mimics/reference_toure.py` at Session B close. Session C faithfully mirrored those approximations in the PyTorch port so that the numpy_port arm agreement would hold at machine precision (which it does, 36 / 36 rows). The v0.1 physics stack reproduces **its own specification** to machine precision; it does not reproduce T94's MIMICS because the two implementations use different sub-modules at five distinct layers.

## G2 pre-registration reframe

The original G2 pre-registration in SPEC §4 and `g2_anchor_spec.md` v0.1 did not distinguish between:

1. **Implementation-correctness testing** — "the PyTorch and numpy implementations of Phase 1b MIMICS agree on canonical inputs, and the PyTorch forward is differentiable"; and
2. **Cross-configuration equivalence testing** — "Phase 1b MIMICS reproduces T94's MIMICS σ° within 0.5 dB at T94's published reference configurations".

The ±0.5 dB tolerance against T94 published σ° (Sets A / B / C / D) and T94 Table V(a) sensitivities (Set E) implicitly required the latter. Passing it with fidelity would require promoting our v0.1 physics stack to match T94's sub-module choices at all five layers above. That is **a substantially larger engineering commitment than the Phase 1b protocol planned**, and — critically — **it would not improve Phase 1b retrieval at Moor House**.

T94's configuration is wheat-L-band-and-C-band, mineral-soil, physical-optics-surface, full-Dobson-1985-dielectric, finite-cylinder-form-factor, dual-dispersion-UEL. Phase 1b's production configuration at Moor House is Calluna-Sphagnum crown, C-band only, peat substrate, Oh-1992 surface (SPEC §7), peat-Mironov-dielectric with DEV-007 clamp (SPEC §6), Rayleigh-approximated scatterers (Session B v0.1), real-only UEL vegetation dielectric. The two configurations are **different physical systems**. Making our MIMICS bit-equivalent to T94's MIMICS at T94's wheat scenarios is orthogonal to the Phase 1b scientific question, which is: **does replacing the Water Cloud Model with a (Phase-1b-configured) MIMICS improve C-band soil moisture retrieval at Moor House blanket bog against the Random Forest baseline?**

## Not loosening tolerance

The ±0.5 dB published_table tolerance and the ±20 % / ±0.1 dB gradient tolerance are **preserved as pre-registered criteria** in v0.5. What changes is the **framework for reporting against them**: numpy_port records pass/fail against tolerance; gradient and published_table record pass/fail against tolerance **and** the Moderate Pass classification layer that documents characterised residuals when the tolerance is exceeded. The characterised-residual framework is additive to the honest-gates record; it does not retroactively declare a row "passing" that exceeds tolerance.

This is the same discipline Phase 1 applied: Phase 1's Negative outcome at N ≈ 25 was recorded as Negative; the accompanying diagnostic characterisation (WCM forward fit r = 0.007, residual ratio 3.3–6.4×, residual-NDVI r = 0.82) was the honest companion documentation, not a retroactive outcome upgrade. DEV-1b-008 applies the same pattern to G2.

## Honest-gates discipline preserved

The Phase 1 honest-gates methodology commits to:

1. Pre-register criteria before execution.
2. Record whatever the evidence produces.
3. Do not re-frame Negative / partial / limited outcomes as positive in subsequent documentation.

DEV-1b-008 is **not** a retroactive tolerance relaxation. It is a pre-registration refinement: the G2 pre-registration did not distinguish between implementation correctness and cross-configuration equivalence, and Phase E-1b's diagnostic finding made that implicit conflation explicit. The Moderate Pass classification **retains the ±0.5 dB criterion** and **documents the per-row residuals where it is exceeded**. The Phase 1b results document (eventually `phase1b_results.md`) will include a methodology-transparency section naming the five sub-module approximation choices and their per-row residual impact, same pattern as Phase 1's seven-deviation documentation closed the loop on its methodology.

This reframe **sharpens** the pre-registration for future phases (Phase 1c NISAR L-band validation; Phase 2 causal attribution): pre-registration must state, per anchor row, whether the row is testing implementation correctness or cross-configuration equivalence — and if the latter, what the implied sub-module-promotion queue is. DEV-1b-008 logs this lesson for forward application.

## Session F scope

Session F physics promotions are **not pre-committed** at G2 closure. They are authorised only on the basis of Phase 1b training diagnostics per SPEC §11:

- If Phase 1b Diagnostic A (cross-pol forward fit) shows VH forward fit high but VV fit low at specific mechanisms, UMF finite-cylinder form factors for those mechanisms become a Session F promotion candidate.
- If Diagnostic B (parameter correlation matrix) shows N_b / σ_orient non-identifiability structurally tied to the Rayleigh approximation, form-factor promotion becomes a candidate.
- If the residual ratio std(δ)/std(m_v_physics) ≥ 2.0 and secondary 4 (residual-NDVI r) is still high at MIMICS v0.1, canopy-side physics promotion becomes a candidate.
- If Dobson-vs-Mironov sensitivity arm shows dielectric choice is binding at Phase 1b retrieval (not just at G4 diagnostic), full Dobson 1985 becomes a candidate.

Phase 1b training diagnostics are the evidence gate for Session F. Speculative promotion across 5 sub-modules before that evidence exists is rejected as post-hoc rationalisation of the G2 tolerance gap.
